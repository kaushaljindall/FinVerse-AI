RESERVE BANK OF INDIA — ANTI-MONEY LAUNDERING GUIDELINES (SIMPLIFIED)

Section 1: Customer Due Diligence (CDD)
1.1 Banks must verify the identity of all customers before establishing a business relationship.
1.2 Enhanced due diligence must be performed for high-risk customers, including politically exposed persons (PEPs).
1.3 Customer identification records must be maintained for a minimum of 5 years after the business relationship has ended.

Section 2: Transaction Monitoring
2.1 Cash transactions exceeding ₹10,00,000 (Ten Lakh Rupees) must be reported to the Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU-IND).
2.2 All series of cash transactions integrally connected to each other which have been valued below ₹10,00,000 but aggregate value exceeds ₹10,00,000 must also be reported.
2.3 Suspicious transactions must be reported regardless of the amount involved.
2.4 A suspicious transaction means a transaction which gives rise to a reasonable ground of suspicion that it may involve the proceeds of an offence.

Section 3: Record Keeping
3.1 Records of all transactions must be maintained for at least 5 years from the date of the transaction.
3.2 Records of customer identification data must be maintained for at least 5 years after the business relationship has ended.

Section 4: Reporting Obligations
4.1 Monthly Cash Transaction Report (CTR) must be filed with FIU-IND for all cash transactions above ₹10,00,000.
4.2 Suspicious Transaction Reports (STR) must be filed within 7 days of arriving at a conclusion that the transaction is suspicious.
4.3 Cross-border wire transfer reports must be filed for all international transfers.

Section 5: Fraud Prevention
5.1 Banks must implement real-time fraud detection systems.
5.2 Unusual patterns such as sudden large transactions, transactions at unusual hours, and transactions from high-risk jurisdictions must be flagged.
5.3 Multiple transactions just below the reporting threshold (structuring) must be identified and reported.

Section 6: Digital Payment Guidelines
6.1 All digital payment transactions must be authenticated with at least two-factor authentication.
6.2 Transaction limits for digital payments: UPI — ₹1,00,000 per transaction; NEFT — No limit; RTGS — Minimum ₹2,00,000.
6.3 Failed transactions must be reversed within T+5 working days.

Section 7: Consumer Protection
7.1 Banks must provide clear disclosure of all charges and fees.
7.2 Interest rate changes must be communicated to customers 30 days in advance.
7.3 Loan agreements must include total cost of credit disclosure.
7.4 Customers have the right to request their credit information from credit bureaus.

Section 8: Investment Advisory Guidelines
8.1 Financial advisors must not guarantee returns on investments.
8.2 Risk factors must be clearly disclosed for all investment products.
8.3 Suitability assessment must be conducted before recommending investment products.
8.4 Past performance disclaimers must be included in all investment recommendations.

Section 9: Insurance Regulations
9.1 Insurance policies must clearly state exclusions and limitations.
9.2 Claim settlement must be completed within 30 days of receiving all required documents.
9.3 Policy cancellation within the free-look period (15 days) must result in full premium refund.
9.4 Mis-selling of insurance products is a punishable offence.

Section 10: Credit Card Regulations
10.1 Credit card billing must include minimum amount due and total outstanding.
10.2 Interest on credit cards cannot exceed the RBI prescribed cap.
10.3 Unsolicited credit cards must not be issued.
10.4 Credit card rewards and benefits must be clearly communicated.
